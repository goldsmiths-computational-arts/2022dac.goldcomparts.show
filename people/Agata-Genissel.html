Agata Genissel

At the forefront of my artist practice lies BRICKWORK: a ‘live’ practice experimenting with technologies that harvest space for error. My work in the exhibition is an iteration of BRICKWORK, as I see different outcomes to exist as different ‘sets’. Indeed, this exercise in fluidity and glitch is dear to my critique of how the digital realm has become an extrapolated tool of control via Late Capitalist power structures. I am hugely intrigued by patterns and repetition as a form of sensemaking and, in the context of using live coding tools such as TidalCycles and Hydra, I feel my imaginative intuitions are given acres to play with by altering patterns, exploring the abundance of creative possibility whilst rejecting the algorithmic force of will. I feel proximate with the machinic mentally and physically, fusing together to create that which can be sensed both outwardly and inwardly, materially and immaterially. The effect scratches at some phenomenological itch where time is suspended between the poles of subjective experience and the happening, giving way to a haptic feedback loop wherein the precarity of the next moment is the very binding agent for this dialectical synthesis. I intend to take BRICKWORK to venue spaces and perform BRICKWORK ‘sets’ in celebration of the technologies and ethos rooted within the audio-visual praxis.

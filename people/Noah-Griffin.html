Noah Griffin

Noah Griffin (born 2000) is an artist based in London, UK. His work explores the potentials found in ambiguity and the exploitation of plot holes in an increasingly systematised social landscape. Focussing on the consequences of ‘normalising functions’ that suppress weirdness and support quantification, Griffin taps into historical and theory driven approaches to generate snapping-out- of moments. His work draws on themes of political subjectification, thought syntax and post-rave culture. In the ongoing project ‘Reform Club’, sound-reactive code is used to edit the movements of dancers projected above the viewers’ heads. A synchronisation is thus generated between music and image, a complication of the cinematic tendency for score to create a holistic whole within which the viewer is entrapped. A narrative relationship is established between the multiple agencies present in the work.

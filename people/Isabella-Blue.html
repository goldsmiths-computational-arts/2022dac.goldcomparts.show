Isabella Blue

Isabella Blue is an artist currently working in London. Their practice revolves around their identity and personal experiences, created using moving image, computation, sculpture and ethnography.Working around the theme identity was a way of starting to understand herself. A learning experience as well as an art practice. It started in 2018 when she looked at art as a way toexpress her difficulties with her emotions and diagnoses. Leading her into working with these topics in the digital realm, which brings us to the 2022 exhibited work called “Online Mutants”.“Online Mutants” is an examination of the different forms we take, depicting identity in two realms, offline and online, which are always changing. Altering the faces in the work, though a mixed media approach of photography, 3d printing and physical computing, is a way of questioning the online self, how it effects our sense of reality and how we are perceived or perceiving others.The personal collection of work is a conversation of identity confusion. Taking experiences and questions the artist struggles with and turning them into conversational pieces for others to contribute to and question themselves.The result is a combination of digital and physical mutants that exist in both realms, aim to provoke critical thinking surrounding the question “who am I?”.

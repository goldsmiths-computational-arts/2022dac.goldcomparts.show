Theo Gofff

I’ve been developing different methods of manipulation and processing influenced by televised sport coverage, home movies and video game graphics among other day to day media formats. I focus on breaking down and then stitching together video that I have either taken myself or are objects of found footage. Recent projects have used computational methods to explore ways that information technology and data collection are changing the social spaces we access. These are both digital and physical spaces occupied by collectives of individuals for mutual benefit. HN7 tries to make some sense of sport’s role in community building activities which exist and thrive today despite increasingly hostile urban environments in the UK’s capital. For the subject of the video, I have used some bodycam footage from a group of local Vietnamese mates I have been working with and documenting. They play a weekly 2 hour, no-halftime game of 8 aside at Southeast London’s Millwall football club which has created a visual direction for this project.

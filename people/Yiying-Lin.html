Yiying Lin

Yiying is a cat-obsessed artist based in London who is interested in cats, obviously. She makes goofy stuff that connects/comments on internet culture, glitch feminism, capital and national identities, kinship and East Asian politics using both digital and traditional materials.  The White Space is a stop motion video that uses the White Cube gallery as its background, featuring an image of the artist's cat sitting in a joss paper bowl representing the artist's cultural and religious background, and her mom's Tiktok contents framed as artworks from a mysterious great artist, all of which are are considered minor or a misfit to the art canon. The work uses Youtube's recommendation feature for interactivity, and can also be seen as an interactive meme. It is a critique of the phenomenon of the White Cube in the western white-dominated art world. As they hold the white space as a sacred neutral space that’s perfect for exhibiting work, the artist challenges that this is not the case in the times that calls for decolonizing the art world. White space doesn’t always represent neutral-ness, but it sometimes neglects local discourses and is limited by the white-dominated worldview.
